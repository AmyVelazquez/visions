How the Craft + Lead A Strategic UX Vision Intensive Works


This Intensive is an online workshop unlike any other you’ve ever experienced. We call it an Intensive because, well, it’s intense.


Yet, once you hear Jared Spool break these new approaches into bite-sized concepts that are easy to understand, you’ll feel like these ideas make complete sense. That’s because they’re a natural next step to your lived experience.


Every moment of every session presents new ideas and explores original ways to approach UX research practice. Past attendees have told us they feel like what they learned has revitalized their careers.

Your minimum commitment: 8½ hours over six days.


The Fundamentals of Strategic UX Visions package consists of one 60-minute session and five 90-minute sessions, each led by Jared Spool. The Prepare to Lead (PTL) a Strategic UX Vision package adds another 60-minute session plus three 90-minute sessions. Altogether, there are fourteen hours of mind-expanding material over the two weeks.


We hold each session twice: at 12pm (16.00 GMT) and 6pm (22.00 GMT). You can attend either session, and Jared will be there to answer your questions.


Plus, each session’s recording is available the next day. That way, if you miss one, you can catch up quickly.


You’ll have questions. We’re ready for that. Each 90-minute session will give us plenty of time to discuss how you’ll make all this work in your situation.


Discuss the gnarliest of challenges, and you’ll come away with all the assurance you need that you’ve got this.

Just the fundamentals? Or are you prepared to lead?


We offer two packages for this intensive to match where you’re at.


The 8½-hour Fundamentals of Strategic UX Visions package is for emerging and existing UX Leaders who want a solid understanding of what goes into a Strategic UX Vision.


Jared Spool will introduce all the essential concepts behind Crafting and Leading a Strategic UX Vision. You’ll discover how to establish the vision, build up the research, communicate the vision, and get buy-in from stakeholders.


The attendance-limited, 14-hour Prepare to Lead (PTL) a Strategic UX Vision package is for UX leaders ready to make this happen in their organizations. You’ll work directly with Jared Spool to create a leadership plan to craft and roll out your strategic UX vision.


These additional sessions cover the implementation details to start your organization’s transformation. We limit the attendance at the PTL sessions, so you’ll get more of Jared’s time to address your particular challenges.


The PTL package also includes a pre-conference session, during which you’ll share your current challenges with Jared. This way, he can tailor the sessions to your needs and supply you with extra resources to give you additional insights into your transformation.


Additionally, as a PTL member, you’ll meet with Jared for a private, 30-minute 1-on-1 Mentoring Session, to address any challenges you encounter once you’ve started applying what you’ve learned.


Note: We won’t offer this Intensive publicly again before the end of 2025.


If you think your organization will need to clarify and define its direction in the next year, you don’t want to miss your opportunity to learn from Jared.


To join the Intensive, decide whether to take in just the fundamentals or the full PTL package.

The roadmap is the pivotal place to show the value of Strategic UX Research.

All significant strategic decisions originate in your organization’s product roadmap. The roadmap determines the direction of your products and services, which determines your organization's direction. As a UX leader, the roadmap is your opportunity to have the most influence over user experiences.

Your Strategic UX Research will shape significant product decisions when you anticipate what those decisions will be. The roadmap is your essential fortune-telling device, predicting the future of every team’s work. As a result, your entire organization becomes more knowledgeable about what your users need when you read the roadmap and discern what it subtly is telling you.

  • Move beyond starting each project from scratch and instead begin with an in-depth knowledge of what your users and customers need.
  • Change your organization’s mindset from “we have to build a thing” to “we have to solve a customer’s problem.”
  • Construct your long-term research agenda from the UX outcomes of each item currently on the roadmap.
  • Add market-leading innovations to the roadmap that come directly from gaps you’ve uncovered in your users’ experiences.
